Integrated Agroforestry and Regenerative Agriculture

Sanjeevan is a collaborative initiative focused on transforming local ecosystems through Integrated Agroforestry and Regenerative Agriculture. Rooted in a systems-based approach, it combines the ecological benefits of agroforestry with the soil-restoring and climate-smart practices of regenerative agriculture. [cite_start]The aim is to build local climate-resilient, biodiverse, and productive landscapes that generate sustainable livelihoods, improve local nutrition, and strengthen market systems. [cite: 5, 6, 7]

What We Specialize In

We work in regions facing acute risks of agricultural collapse, livelihood loss, and nutritional insecurity. [cite_start]The core challenge is regenerating and productively using existing resources, moving away from extractive practices toward restorative, circular approaches. [cite: 3, 4]

Our Approach

Reinforce conservation and productivity

Our integrated agroforestry model is anchored around local water sources. [cite_start]We build localized ecosystems that sustain themselves, enhancing water efficiency, improving yields, and contributing to better nutrition and income at the community level. [cite: 16, 17]

Access to marketplace

We build market systems by enabling knowledge, access, and connection through structured marketplace mechanisms. Our strategy includes product tracing, value addition, and aligning with market demand. [cite_start]These interventions also generate income by enabling access to carbon markets. [cite: 19, 20, 21, 22]

Enabling technology adoption

[cite_start]

Solar power is a key enabler, providing reliable energy for water pumps, drip irrigation, soil moisture sensors, and more. [cite: 24]

Implementation Model Components

[cite_start]

Our approach integrates agroforestry, aquaculture, and livestock systems with regenerative paddy cultivation to enhance productivity and resilience, especially during the dry season. [cite: 58, 59]

    [cite_start]
  • Homestead Agroforestry: Utilizes natural resources to support nutrition, income, and ecological resilience at the household level. [cite: 60, 61]
    • [cite_start]
  • Farm-Based Agroforestry: Brings together tree cultivation with intercrops for larger land parcels to boost ecological and economic outcomes. [cite: 62, 63]
    • [cite_start]
  • Regenerative Paddy Cultivation: Transforms traditional rice farming with water-saving methods to improve year-round productivity and link food security with climate resilience. [cite: 64, 65]
